Optimize Water Distribution

Hello dear coder. We meet again. Today we are going to solve another question from graph theory, "Optimize Water Distribution". Don't forget to check out the problem first.

Don't forget to check out the problem first.

Optimize Water Distribution- Problem link

PROBLEM DISCUSSION

  • There are n construction sites in a town. We want to supply water for all the construction sites by building wells and laying pipes.
  • For each site i, we can either build a well inside it directly with cost of wells[i-1], or pipe in water from another well to it.
  • The costs to lay pipes between sites are given by the 2d array cost, where each row of cost contains 3 numbers ai, bi and wi where wi is the cost to connect ai to bi. These connections are bidirectional.
  • We have to return the minimum total cost to supply water to all the construction sites.

We understand this problem for figure 1.

Here, we are given 5 construction sites which are denoted by the vertices 1 to 5. Against each vertex, the cost to build a well at that construction site is given. The edges between the vertices represent the pipelines/connections between them. The numbers beside the edges denote the cost required to connect the 2 edges.

Now we are required to find out the minimum cost to supply water at all the construction sites.

APPROACH

We know to solve this problem, at least one well must be dug at some construction site so that it can supply water to the other sites through pipelines.

Reader, you know that if we did not have the problem of digging any well and the water was supplied just through pipelines, then the minimum cost could be calculated through Minimum Spanning Tree (MST).

However, since at least one well is to be dug, we make some modifications in our graph to apply MST on it.

In figure 2, we add a dummy node, the 0th construction site and from this node we connect every other construction site. This node contains a combined well from which water can be supplied to the other sites.

Now the cost of supplying water from this 0th node to any other node through pipelines is equivalent to the given cost of digging a well in the other node.

This reduced problem can now easily be solved through Minimum Spanning Tree.

We suggest you watch the solution video of Minimum Spanning Tree, if you haven't already.

Hence, this problem is just an application of MST.

CODE

Reader, if you know how to solve the Prim's Algorithm (i.e. Minimum Spanning Tree) and have carefully studied the approach then you will have no trouble in coding this question.

TIME & SPACE COMPLEXITY
TIME COMPLEXITY- O(E+VlogV)

Time Complexity is similar to Dijkstra's algorithm, since we have just replaced the weight so far with the current edge weight. Hence, total time complexity will be O(E + VlogV) where E = number of edges and V = number of vertices.

SPACE COMPLEXITY- O(N)

We are using a priority queue, which will take O(N) space, where N is equal to the maximum pair objects pushed, and is equivalent to the number of vertices. Also, we have declared a visited array of size N.
